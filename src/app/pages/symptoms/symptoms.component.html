Common Symptoms

Persistent cough with mucus

A chronic cough that produces phlegm is often an early sign of COPD, caused by airway inflammation and mucus buildup.

Shortness of breath, especially with activity

Difficulty breathing during physical exertion occurs as lung function declines and airflow becomes restricted.

Wheezing

A high-pitched whistling sound while breathing, caused by narrowed or obstructed airways due to inflammation.

Chest tightness

A feeling of pressure or heaviness in the chest, often resulting from trapped air and reduced airflow in the lungs.

Frequent respiratory infections

People with COPD are more prone to infections like bronchitis or pneumonia, which worsen symptoms and lung damage.

Immediate Action Required

If you are experiencing one or more of the symptoms below, you should take urgent action and have the necessary examinations.

Early diagnosis of COPD is crucial because it slows disease progression, improves quality of life, reduces flare-ups, supports smoking cessation, prevents complications, and enables more effective, less costly treatment.

Sudden chest pain or shortness of breath

May indicate a pneumothorax (collapsed lung), which requires immediate medical attention to prevent further respiratory distress.

Fever or increased cough

Could be a sign of a respiratory infection such as pneumonia or bronchitis, especially concerning if symptoms worsen rapidly.

Increased mucus or blood in sputum

May suggest an infection, inflammation, or bleeding in the lungs or airways, and should be evaluated promptly.

Drop in oxygen levels

Indicates impaired lung function or oxygen exchange, potentially leading to hypoxia; urgent assessment and intervention are needed.
