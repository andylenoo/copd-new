Treatments of COPD

The treatment of COPD focuses on relieving symptoms, improving quality of life, slowing disease progression, and preventing complications. While COPD is a chronic condition with no cure, a comprehensive treatment plan can help patients breathe better and stay active.

Lifestyle Changes

  • Smoking cessation (most critical)
  • Avoiding lung irritants (pollution, dust, chemicals)
  • Pulmonary rehabilitation (exercise, education, nutrition)

Medications

  • Bronchodilators (e.g., albuterol, salmeterol): relax airway muscles
  • Inhaled corticosteroids (e.g., fluticasone): reduce inflammation
  • Combination inhalers (LABA + ICS or LABA + LAMA)
  • Phosphodiesterase-4 inhibitors (e.g., roflumilast): reduce inflammation
  • Theophylline (less commonly used)
  • Antibiotics (for bacterial exacerbations)

Oxygen Therapy

  • For patients with low blood oxygen levels

Surgical Options

  • Lung volume reduction surgery
  • Bullectomy
  • Lung transplant (in severe cases)

Vaccinations

  • Influenza, pneumococcal, and COVID-19 vaccines to prevent infections

Management of Exacerbations

  • Increased bronchodilators
  • Oral corticosteroids
  • Short-term antibiotics
  • Hospitalization if severe

Nutritional Support

  • Especially in advanced disease stages

Psychological Support

  • For anxiety, depression common in COPD patients

COPD Balloon Treatment

COPD balloon treatment, also known as "desquamation" or "Broncho Muco Cleaner," is an innovative bronchoscopic procedure primarily used in Turkey for patients with chronic bronchitis, a component of COPD, characterized by airway narrowing and thickening of goblet cells due to tobacco exposure.

Target
  • The treatment focuses on removing goblet cells from the inner layer of accessible respiratory pathways, primarily airways ranging from 3 to 8 mm in diameter. Goblet cells are responsible for excessive mucus production, which contributes to airway obstruction in chronic bronchitis.
Procedure
  • A bronchoscope is inserted through an intubation tube into the patient's airways.
  • COPD balloons, chosen to match the diameter of the bronchi, are advanced through the bronchoscope.
  • These balloons, made of polyisoprene and covered with a thermoplastic polystyrene weave (or a latex-free mesh), are inflated and deflated with adjustable pressure and frequency using an electronic pump.
  • The scraping action of the balloon on the thickened inner layer of the bronchi is intended to restore it to its original thickness. This process, termed "desquamation," aims to transform the thickened goblet cell layer into a foamy liquid.
  • The foamy liquid, containing the removed goblet cells, is then aspirated through the bronchoscope channel and expelled.
Goal
  • The primary objective is to relieve chronic bronchitis caused by smoking and restore the altered bronchial epithelium layer, thereby improving airway width and reducing mucus production.
Key Features
  • Mechanical Solution: It's presented as a mechanical solution without the placement of foreign bodies in the airway.
  • Painless and Non-invasive: Claims are made that the procedure is painless, non-invasive, and allows for an immediate return to daily life.
  • Availability: Currently, this specific "COPD balloon treatment" or "Broncho Muco Cleaner" appears to be primarily available in Turkey.
