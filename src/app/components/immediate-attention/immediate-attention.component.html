Don’t Ignore These COPD Symptoms — Get Evaluated Urgently

Early diagnosis of COPD is crucial because it slows disease progression, improves quality of life, reduces flare-ups, supports smoking cessation, prevents complications, and enables more effective, less costly treatment.

1. Sudden chest pain or shortness of breath

May indicate a pneumothorax (collapsed lung), which requires immediate medical attention to prevent further respiratory distress.

2. Fever or increased cough

Could be a sign of a respiratory infection such as pneumonia or bronchitis, especially concerning if symptoms worsen rapidly.

3. Increased mucus or blood in sputum

May suggest an infection, inflammation, or bleeding in the lungs or airways, and should be evaluated promptly.

4. Drop in oxygen levels

Indicates impaired lung function or oxygen exchange, potentially leading to hypoxia; urgent assessment and intervention are needed.

If you are experiencing one or more of the symptoms above, you should take urgent action and have the necessary examinations.
